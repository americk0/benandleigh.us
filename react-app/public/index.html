Our Story

Leigh

My first memory of Ben is from 7th grade chorus — I came home and told my mom about “some dumb kid from school” who picked going to a rock climbing competition over participating in All State choir. However, we didn’t really get to know each other until freshman year of high school, when a mutual friend invited us to a birthday party. I thought he looked so cool in his backwards baseball cap, and his great sense of humor didn’t hurt. After a night of pizza hut, bowling, and more of his jokes, I knew I had a crush, and we started dating a few months later when he asked me to homecoming. We ended up choosing colleges four hours apart, and we took the leap of faith and decided to try long distance (I already knew I wanted to marry him someday). We made it work, even when that meant scheduling time for skyping and phone calls around our busy schedules. I couldn’t ask for a more uplifting partner. He’s my shoulder to cry on, and he supports all of my dreams, including the one where I want to own 32 cats and dogs. Even after eight years together, I’m still amazed by how passionate, smart, easy going, and goofy he is, and I’m so excited to spend the rest of our lives loving each other and growing together.

Ben

In spring of high school freshman year, I got a call from a mutual friend to ask if I wanted to attend a dinner-and-bowling birthday party that night. I was the last to arrive at Pizza Hut but there was an empty seat across from Leigh. I had a crush as soon as I saw her and was amazed at how I had missed such a beautiful girl for so many years, but as crushes typically go, it took me a couple months to work up the courage to ask her out. I set up another bowling night one fall weekend just to have an excuse to see her again and see if we hit it off. I asked her out on Monday. I was so happy that I rode my bike home before I realized I had skipped a chorus rehearsal and had to ride back. Since then, Leigh has continued to change my life and help shape who I am, and I love her more everyday. In the last 8 years we've made it through high school, college, and starting a career despite long-distance and 4-hour daily commutes and our bond is stronger for it. Leigh is a wonderful, beautiful, brilliant, creative person and my absolute best friend, and I can't wait to start the next chapter of our life.
